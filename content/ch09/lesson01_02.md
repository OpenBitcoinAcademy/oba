## Why Fees Exist

Each block has limited space. Bitcoin produces roughly one block every 10 minutes. The block weight limit is 4 million weight units (about 1 to 1.5 MB of data depending on transaction types).

When more transactions are broadcast than a single block can hold, miners choose which to include. The fee market emerges from this scarcity: users who need faster confirmation pay higher fees. Users who can wait pay less.

Fees also provide long-term security. As the block subsidy decreases with each halving, transaction fees become a larger share of miner revenue. After approximately 2140, fees will be the sole incentive for miners to produce blocks.
