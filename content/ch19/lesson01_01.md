## The Layer Stack

Bitcoin operates on multiple layers. Layer 1 is the blockchain: the final settlement layer where consensus rules are enforced by every full node. Transactions on Layer 1 are permanent, auditable, and secured by proof of work.

Layer 2 protocols build on top of Layer 1 without changing it. The Lightning Network is the most deployed Layer 2. It moves payments off-chain into bilateral channels, using Layer 1 only for opening, closing, and dispute resolution.

Beyond Layer 2, application-specific protocols use Bitcoin transactions as anchors for their own state machines. RGB, Fedimint, and Ark each take a different approach to extending Bitcoin's capabilities while inheriting its security guarantees.
