Tagged hashes are a defense-in-depth measure. Without domain separation, an attacker could find inputs where a TapTweak hash collides with a TapLeaf hash, potentially tricking the verifier into accepting invalid scripts. Tagged hashes make this cryptographically infeasible. Every BIP 340/341 hash operation uses a unique tag: "TapTweak", "TapLeaf", "TapBranch", "TapSighash", "BIP0340/challenge".
