## Three Purposes of a Digital Signature

A digital signature in Bitcoin serves three purposes.

**Authorization.** The signature proves that the controller of the private key authorized the spending of funds. Without the private key, no valid signature can be produced.

**Nonrepudiation.** Once a signature is broadcast, the signer cannot deny having authorized the transaction. The signature is mathematical proof of intent.

**Integrity.** The signature commits to the transaction data. If any part of the transaction changes after signing, the signature becomes invalid. No third party can alter the transaction without invalidating the signature.
