## Physical Storage and Hardware Wallets

For long-term storage, keep private keys offline. A hardware signing device (hardware wallet) stores keys in a tamper-resistant chip that never exposes them to the connected computer. Transactions are signed on the device and only the signed transaction leaves it.

For the seed phrase backup, use durable physical media. Paper degrades. Metal plates stamped with the seed words survive fire and water. Store backups in separate physical locations to protect against theft or natural disaster.

Cold storage (keys that have never touched an internet-connected device) provides the strongest protection against remote attacks.
