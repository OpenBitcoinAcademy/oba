## The Root of Trust

Every security system has a root of trust: the one component that must be trusted for everything else to work. In a bank, the root of trust is the bank itself. In Bitcoin, the root of trust is the consensus rules enforced by your full node.

If you run your own full node, you verify every rule independently. Your root of trust is the software on your own hardware. If you rely on someone else's node, your root of trust shifts to that third party.

Hardware wallets add a second root of trust for key security: the tamper-resistant chip that holds your private keys. The full node verifies the blockchain. The hardware wallet protects the keys. Together, they form a security model where no single point of compromise loses everything.
