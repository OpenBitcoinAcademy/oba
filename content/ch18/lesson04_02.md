## Cooperative and Force Close

A cooperative close happens when both parties agree on the final balance. They sign a single closing transaction that pays each party directly, with no timelocks. Immediate settlement, lowest fees.

A force close (unilateral close) happens when one party broadcasts their latest commitment transaction without the other's cooperation. The initiator's funds are locked behind a timelock (days to weeks). The other party can claim their funds immediately. Used when the counterparty is offline or unresponsive.
