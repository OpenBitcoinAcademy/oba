## Fees and Mining Pools

As the block subsidy shrinks, transaction fees become a larger share of miner revenue. When the subsidy reaches zero, fees will be the only incentive to mine. Bitcoin's long-term security depends on a healthy fee market.

Solo mining is impractical for most participants today. The probability of a single machine finding a block is vanishingly small. Mining pools solve this by letting thousands of miners combine their hash power and share rewards proportionally.

Pool members submit partial proofs of work (hashes that meet a lower difficulty than the real target). These shares prove each miner is contributing computation. When the pool finds a valid block, it distributes the reward based on each member's share count. The pool operator takes a small percentage as a fee. Miners receive smaller, steadier payouts instead of rare, large ones.
