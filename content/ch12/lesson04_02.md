## Mining Centralization Risk

Mining pools coordinate the work of many individual miners. A pool operator constructs the candidate block and distributes work units. Individual miners contribute hash power but do not choose which transactions to include.

If a small number of pools control a majority of hash power, those operators gain outsized influence over transaction ordering and block content. They could censor transactions or coordinate an attack. This is a centralization risk, not a certainty. Pool members can switch pools at any time, and new protocols like Stratum V2 give individual miners more control over block template construction.

The economic incentives work against sustained centralization. A pool that censors transactions or attempts an attack damages confidence in Bitcoin, reducing the value of the rewards it earns. Rational miners leave misbehaving pools.
