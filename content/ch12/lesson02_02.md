## The Halving and the 21 Million Cap

The block subsidy started at 50 BTC per block in January 2009. Every 210,000 blocks (roughly four years), it halves. The first halving in November 2012 reduced the subsidy to 25 BTC. The second in July 2016 cut it to 12.5 BTC. The third in May 2020 brought it to 6.25 BTC. The fourth in April 2024 reduced it to 3.125 BTC.

This geometric series converges to a maximum of 21 million bitcoin. No protocol rule states "21 million" directly. The cap emerges from the halving schedule: $50 \times 210{,}000 \times \sum_{i=0}^{\infty} (1/2)^i = 50 \times 210{,}000 \times 2 = 21{,}000{,}000$. Around the year 2140, the subsidy will round down to zero satoshis.

The halving creates a predictable, declining issuance curve. Every participant can verify the current supply by summing all coinbase outputs. No central bank, no discretionary policy.
