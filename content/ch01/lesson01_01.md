## What is Money?

Money is anything people agree to use as payment. Throughout history, that has taken many forms.

Cowrie shells served as currency across Africa and Asia for thousands of years. Gold coins replaced them in many places because gold is scarce, durable, and divisible. Paper notes came next: promises backed by gold stored in vaults.

Today, most money is digital. Numbers in a bank's database. When you pay for something with a card, no physical object moves. Your bank subtracts a number from your account and adds it to someone else's.

This works because you trust the bank.
