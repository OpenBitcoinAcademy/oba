## SHA-256 in Bitcoin

Bitcoin uses a hash function called SHA-256, designed by the United States National Security Agency. SHA stands for Secure Hash Algorithm. 256 refers to the output size: 256 bits, or 32 bytes.

SHA-256 appears everywhere in Bitcoin. Transaction IDs are double-SHA-256 hashes. Mining involves finding inputs that produce hashes below a target value. Addresses are derived from hashes of public keys.

When you hear "hash" in Bitcoin, assume SHA-256 unless told otherwise.
