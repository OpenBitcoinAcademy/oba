## The Double-Spending Problem

Digital information is easy to copy. If you email a photo, you still have the photo. Both of you now have identical copies.

Money cannot work this way. If you send someone 10 units, you should no longer have those 10 units. Otherwise you could spend the same money twice.

Banks solve this by keeping a central ledger. They track who owns what. When you send money, the bank updates its records.

Bitcoin solves it differently. Instead of one bank keeping the ledger, thousands of computers around the world keep identical copies. They agree on every transaction through a process called consensus. No single entity controls the record.
