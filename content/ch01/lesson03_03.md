## Signatures and Verification

Your private key is like a master signature that only you can produce. Your public key is like a stamp that anyone can use to verify your signature.

When you send bitcoin, you create a digital signature using your private key and the transaction data. Anyone on the network can check this signature against your public key to confirm that you authorized the transaction.

The signature proves ownership without revealing the private key. You can sign a million transactions and your private key remains secret.
