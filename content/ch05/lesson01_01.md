## Wallets Hold Keys, Not Coins

A Bitcoin wallet does not contain bitcoin. It contains private keys. Those keys authorize spending bitcoin recorded on the blockchain.

The blockchain records which outputs are locked to which public key hashes. Your wallet tracks which of those public key hashes correspond to private keys it controls. When someone says "I have 2 BTC in my wallet," they mean the blockchain has unspent outputs totaling 2 BTC that are spendable with keys their wallet holds.

Losing access to the private keys means losing the ability to spend. There is no password reset, no customer support, and no way to recover keys that are gone.
