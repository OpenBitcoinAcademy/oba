## Types of Wallets

**Software wallets** run on phones, desktops, or servers. They store keys in encrypted files on the device.

**Hardware wallets** are dedicated devices that store private keys in tamper-resistant chips. They sign transactions internally and never expose the private key to the connected computer.

**Paper wallets** are private keys printed or written on physical media. They are offline by nature, which protects against remote attacks but is vulnerable to physical loss or damage.

Each type makes a different trade-off between convenience and security. Most users benefit from a hardware wallet for savings and a software wallet for daily spending.
