## Backup Strategies

The seed phrase is the most critical backup. Write it on paper or stamp it into metal. Store it in a secure location. Do not photograph it, email it, or store it in any digital format connected to the internet.

Beyond the seed, wallets may store nonkey data: transaction labels, address descriptions, fee preferences. This metadata cannot be recovered from the seed alone. Some wallets offer encrypted backups that include this data.

A solid backup strategy: store the seed phrase in two separate physical locations (protection against fire, flood, or theft of one copy). Test the recovery process by restoring from the seed on a separate device before trusting it with real funds.
