## Confirmations

Once a transaction is included in a block, it has one confirmation. Each subsequent block added on top increases the confirmation count.

One confirmation means a miner included your transaction. Six confirmations (about one hour) is the traditional threshold for high-value payments. At that depth, reversing the transaction would require rewriting six blocks, which demands more computing power than any attacker is likely to have.

For small payments, one or two confirmations are sufficient. Some merchants accept zero-confirmation transactions for low-value purchases, accepting the small risk that the transaction could be replaced before confirmation.
